Common questions

Frequently asked questions

What is a MARA registered migration agent?
A registered migration agent (RMA) is a person registered under the Migration Agents Registration Authority (MARA) to provide immigration assistance in Australia. Only registered agents and legal practitioners can lawfully charge fees for immigration advice. Dimitri Bourne is registered under LPN 5511816.
How long does a visa application take?
Processing times vary significantly by visa type. Partner visas (820/801) can take 12-36 months. Skilled independent visas (189/190) typically take 3-12 months from invitation. Business migration visas (188 stream) can take 12-24 months. We will provide realistic timeframes after assessing your specific circumstances.
Do you handle regional and state-sponsored visas?
Yes. We assist with state-nominated visas (190), regional sponsored visas (491), and employer-sponsored regional work visas. We also manage Expressions of Interest (EOI) through SkillSelect and advise on maximising your points score.
Can you help if I have had a visa refused or cancelled?
Yes. We handle Ministerial Intervention requests, Administrative Appeals Tribunal (AAT) reviews, and Federal Court appeals. Refusals and cancellations are serious -- contact us immediately as strict time limits apply.
What legal services do you offer alongside immigration?
As a full-service law firm, we offer conveyancing, civil litigation, employment law, wills and estate planning, family law (including prenuptial agreements), commercial law, criminal and traffic matters, and dispute resolution -- all from the same firm.
Do you offer fixed-fee pricing?
Where possible, yes. Our self-service products (letter of demand, will kit, statutory declaration, etc.) are fixed-fee. Immigration and litigation matters are quoted per engagement after an initial consultation. All fees are disclosed in a costs agreement before work commences, in compliance with Queensland Law Society requirements.
Do you assist clients from China, PNG, and the Pacific?
Yes -- these are core client groups for Bourne Law. We have extensive experience with Chinese business migration clients (188A-E) and Pacific engagement visas (192). Chinese-language documentation is available for business migration matters.
